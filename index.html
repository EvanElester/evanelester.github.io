Artificial Love

https://youtu.be/ZX6-xLoUPWU

This animated short asks a question that I believe will soon be relevant to all of us: is artificial intelligence capable of love? Are these robots actually forming relationships or simply mimicking the behavior of their creators? I created the robots by modeling and creating the skeleton in Maya, texturing in Substance Painter, and rigging in Unreal Engine. This film was rendered and animated in Unreal Engine and edited in Premiere Pro.

Book asset: "Medieval Open Book 2" (https://skfb.ly/6WOpY) by J0Y is licensed under Creative Commons Attribution (http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/). https://sketchfab.com/3d-models/medieval-open-book-2-3bbd0f6acc074ca29437cc14cee0990a

Sound: windy forest.mp3 by inchadney -- https://freesound.org/s/151823/ -- License: Attribution 4.0 https://freesound.org/people/inchadney/sounds/151823/

 

Software Used: Unreal Engine, Maya, Substance 3D Painter, Premiere, PureRef

Desert Campsite

https://youtu.be/8qu1OoTiOSo

Rendered in Unreal Engine. The props were modeled in Maya and textured in Substance 3D Painter, and the cloth section of the hammock was sculpted in ZBrush. I used assets from the Quixel library in Unreal Engine to build the desert environment. Music was created by me.

The robot was partially inspired by eyebots from Fallout.

Software Used: Unreal Engine, Maya, ZBrush, Substance 3D Painter, PureRef

The Temple of Four Pioneers

The prompt for this project was to embed narrative details into an interior scene through the choice of objects, placement of objects, style of décor, lighting, placement of the camera, etc. For some of the renders, I took inspiration from the lighting in Nier:Automata.

Software Used: Maya, ZBrush, Substance 3D Painter, Photoshop, PureRef

Sorcery! Animated Opening

https://youtu.be/4gkDs6ANfr4

This animated opening acts as a concept for a show based on Steve Jackson’s Sorcery! adventure gamebook series. This project exclusively displays my editing skills. All images in this video are AI generated still images. I developed the concept, added motion and effects, and managed the pacing so that it fits with the music.

 

Software Used: Premiere Pro
